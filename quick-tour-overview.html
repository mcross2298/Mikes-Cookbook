Mike's Cookbook · The Big Picture

Heirloom recipes & performance plates, in your pocket

Mike's Cookbook is a tactile, installable cooking app built for the kitchen. Whole-food, low-carb recipes on a two-meals-a-day rhythm, every dish scalable to any serving count, per-serving macros on tap, a weekly planner that merges into one smart grocery list, and a built-in macro tracker with barcode lookup. This page is the full end-to-end — read it once and you'll know exactly what the app can do. Sign in and it also links up with the 4 Weeks to Open workout app — one account, so your planned meals and training day both show up on the other app's Home screen.

Home — the hub & spokes

A hub-and-spoke app with no bottom tab bar. Home is the hub; you open a spoke screen and a “‹ Home” arrow always brings you back, so you can't get lost.

  • ✓This Week hero — the weekly meal planner, front and center, reflecting your live plan.
  • ✓Browse modules — Categories, Recipes, Macro Tracker, Mike's Favorites, your saved Favorites, and Add Recipe, each a tap away.
  • ✓Uniform back-nav — every spoke carries a “‹ Home” arrow; recipe and collection pages get a floating 🏠 Home button.
  • ✓Deep-linkable — the active screen mirrors to the URL (e.g. #recipes, #tracker), so reloads and shared links land in the right place.
  • ✓🏋️ Workout app link — the icon beside search opens 4 Weeks to Open; one sign-in works in both apps.

3
Serving scaling & the macro view

The heart of every recipe page: scale the dish to your table and read it three ways across swipeable tabs.

  • ✓Serving stepper (1–12) — recipes are authored at 2 and 4 servings; any other count is scaled live from the native tier, rescaling every ingredient quantity on the fly.
  • ✓Per-serving macros — the Overview & Macros tab shows calories and the protein / fat / carb breakdown per single serving, constant no matter how many you cook for.
  • ✓Clean grocery list — the Grocery tab groups items by aisle (Meat · Dairy · Produce · Pantry) as a clean shopping list — no fiddly exact amounts, because you buy standard packages.
  • ✓Step-by-step method — the Recipe tab carries the numbered instructions with per-ingredient prep notes. Swipe between all three tabs.
  • ✓Check-off state per serving — tick groceries and steps as you go; progress is saved on-device, keyed to each recipe and serving count.
  • ✓Cooking Mode — ▸ Start Cooking on the Recipe tab opens a full-screen, one-step-at-a-time view (screen kept awake) with an opt-in 🎙️ voice control toggle: "next step," "previous step," "read ingredients" (spoken aloud), all hands-free.

Cook for one tonight and six on Sunday from the same recipe — the quantities follow you, and the macros stay honest per plate.

4
This Week planner & grocery list

Plan the week's meals and the app turns them into one smart shopping trip.

  • ✓Build the week — add the recipes you're cooking and the planner tracks your meals and recipe count from the Home hero.
  • ✓One combined grocery list — every planned recipe's ingredients merged and grouped by aisle, so you shop once for the whole week.
  • ✓Check off as you shop — the plan remembers which items are already in the cart.
  • ✓✨ Smart Week — auto-suggests a 5-meal combo whose ingredients overlap, so perishable Produce, Meat and Dairy get used up instead of going to waste. Shuffle for another combo, or add all 5 in one tap. Signed in with the workout app linked, it also leans higher-protein on your real established training days and lighter on rest days, learned from your logged workout history.
  • ✓⏱️ Time Check — a precursor to Smart Week: assign each day Quick (<30 min), Standard (~1 hr), or No rush, and it generates the whole day-by-day meal grid to match your schedule.
5
The macro tracker

A full day-tracker built into the cookbook, so eating and cooking live side by side.

  • ✓Daily macro dashboard — calories, protein, fat and carbs tracked against your goals, logged by the hour, with a tappable day calendar.
  • ✓Food search + barcode scanner — look up foods in a large database, or scan a label: the scanner detects the barcode and pulls the nutrition in automatically. No match or offline? It falls back to manual entry.
  • ✓Tappable nutrition-facts sheet — full macro breakdown and micronutrients, a grams / oz unit toggle, and a keypad for a custom quantity or multiple. Macros recalculate live as you adjust.
  • ✓Favorite foods — star the staples and quick-log them in a couple of taps; log a cookbook recipe straight into your day, too.
  • ✓Optional sign-in, cross-device sync — tap the 👤 icon on Home to sign in with an invited account, and your tracker, meal plan, macro history and My Recipes follow you across devices, reconciling with the same account in the 4 Weeks to Open workout app if you use both. Stay signed out and everything keeps working exactly as before, fully offline on this device.
  • ✓Manual backup file — the same 👤 sheet has Export data / Import data, so even without an account you have a way to save and restore your cookbook data.
6
Make it yours

Save what you love and add your own — your recipes behave exactly like the built-ins.

  • ✓Personal Favorites — tap the ❤ on any recipe (Home, collection, or detail — they all share one list) and it collects on the Home Favorites card.
  • ✓Mike's Favorites — a curated, shipped-to-everyone shortlist of the dishes Mike has actually made and loved, distinct from your personal hearts.
  • ✓Add your own recipe — create a dish or save a family hand-me-down into your library; it's fully searchable, categorized, plannable, and favoritable like every built-in recipe.
7
Offline-first, on your device

Built to work at the counter with flaky Wi-Fi, with everything saved locally.

  • ✓Installs like a native app — Add to Home Screen and it launches full-screen, no browser bar. A service worker caches the app so recipes, scaling, grocery lists and macros keep working offline.
  • ✓On-device first — favorites, your weekly plan, grocery check-offs, recipe check-off state, your added recipes, and tracker logs all save locally. Private and instant.
  • ✓Seamless updates — the app self-activates new versions and an update toast lets you refresh, so fixes reach you without a manual reinstall.
8
The look — warm, tactile & premium

A design built for a phone held in floury hands.

Deep slate surfaces carry warm cream recipe cards and serif headings for a heirloom-cookbook feel. Terracotta is the signature accent — calls to action, active states, and recipe theming — with muted sage as the calm second voice. Each recipe can carry its own accent, theming its detail page end to end. Large tap targets, swipeable tabs, and reduced-motion / safe-area support keep it comfortable in a real kitchen.
